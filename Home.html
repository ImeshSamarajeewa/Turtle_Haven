Welcome to Turtle Haven
Sri Lanka

Creating a better place for the turtles who are in need of the support from us humans

Sri Lankan turtle hatcheries establish serene sanctuaries dedicated to the protection and censervation of endangered sea turtles. These peaceful havens provide a safe space for turtles, ensuring the survival of their eggs and the subsequents release of baby turtles into the ocean. By offering a peaceful environment and fostering sustainable practices, these hatchries play a vital role in presserving these remarkable creatures and raising awarness about the impotance of marine conservation. By safeguarding nesting grounds, rescuing injured turtles, and promoting sustainable practices, these hatcheries contribute to the long-term survival of sea turtle populations. These turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka provide a valuable platform for research, education, and community involvement in turtle conservation efforts. By visiting these hatcheries, individuals can learn about the different turtle species, their life cycles, and the challenges they face. These initiatives not only contribute to the conservation of sea turtles but also support the sustainable development of coastal communities and the preservation of Sri Lanka's rich biodiversity.

Our mission

Our mission as a turtle hatchery in Sri Lanka is to protect and conserve these magnificent creatures and their fragile habitats. With the alarming decline in turtle populations, we strive to make a difference by providing a safe haven for these endangered species. At our hatchery, we are dedicated to the rescue and rehabilitation of sea turtles, focusing on the four major species found in Sri Lankan waters: the Green Turtle, Leatherback Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Olive Ridley Turtle. Our primary goal is to ensure their survival and promote sustainable practices to mitigate the threats they face. We actively engage in the protection of nesting sites, monitoring nesting patterns, and implementing stringent measures to combat poaching and illegal trade. Our dedicated team of experts and volunteers work tirelessly to collect eggs from vulnerable nesting grounds and transfer them to our hatchery, providing a protected environment for their incubation. Once hatched, we release the baby turtles into the ocean, increasing their chances of survival.

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a conservation center located in Hikkaduwa, a coastal town in Sri Lanka. It is dedicated to the protection and preservation of sea turtles, as well as the conservation of their natural habitat. The hatchery plays a crucial role in raising awareness about the importance of turtle conservation and conducting research on these magnificent creatures.

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a renowned conservation center located in Kosgoda, a coastal village in Sri Lanka. It is dedicated to the protection and conservation of sea turtles, with a particular focus on the endangered species found in the region. The hatchery plays a vital role in raising awareness, conducting research, and actively working to conserve these fascinating creatures and their habitats

The loggerhead sea turtle, scientifically known as Caretta caretta, is a large marine turtle that belongs to the family Cheloniidae. It is named for its relatively large head, which is equipped with strong jaws that allow it to feed on a variety of prey. Loggerhead sea turtles play a significant role in marine ecosystems as both predators and prey.
